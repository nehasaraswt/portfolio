## Content Writing Checklist

### Challenge Section
- [ ] Describe 247.ai and what it does (context for reader)
- [ ] Explain the design problem: AI interactions are new, no established patterns
- [ ] Mention team state: inexperienced with AI design, uncertain approach
- [ ] State the goal: create sustainable system for AI products

**Example opening**:
```
247.ai is a [product description]. The core innovation is its AI 
engine that [capability]. But there's a design problem: how do you 
design interactions with AI when best practices haven't been established?

When I joined as Design Director, the team was:
- Designers with strong traditional UX skills
- But zero experience designing for AI interactions
- Stakeholders debating how much to surface AI reasoning to users
- Timeline pressure to ship features quickly

I needed to establish a design system that could support rapid feature
development while teaching the team how to think about AI interactions.
```

### Approach Section
- [ ] Explain research: what did you learn about AI interactions?
- [ ] Describe the design system framework you created
- [ ] Show team capability-building process
- [ ] Connect to emerging tech strategy

**Example**:
```
I took a three-pronged approach:

1. RESEARCH
   Studied leading AI products (OpenAI's ChatGPT, Anthropic's Claude, etc.)
   to identify emerging patterns for AI interactions. Conducted user research
   to understand mental models: how do people expect AI to behave?

2. DESIGN SYSTEM
   Created a modular design system specifically for AI interactions:
   - AI Response Container (how AI outputs are presented)
   - Confidence Indicators (how to show uncertainty)
   - Reasoning Visibility (how much to show the "why" behind decisions)
   - Error & Fallback States (graceful handling of AI failures)

3. TEAM CAPABILITY
   Rather than just handing designers a system, I ran workshops where
   team learned *why* each pattern existed. Created "AI Design Principles"
   they could use to make decisions when patterns weren't obvious yet.
```

### Results Section
- [ ] Quantify product impact (feature velocity, user adoption)
- [ ] Quantify team impact (confidence, consistency, speed)
- [ ] Show design system metrics (coverage, reuse, updates needed)
- [ ] Connect results back to your strategic approach

**Example metrics**:
```
DESIGN SYSTEM IMPACT
─────────────────────────────────
Design System Coverage                    94%
Feature Velocity (design ready)    2 weeks faster
Design Consistency Score (audit)            92%
Designers' Confidence (self-report)       4.1/5
AI Feature User Adoption                   67%
Time to implement new AI feature     -35% faster
Team Retention (sticky team)               98%
```

Together with Walmart, these two cases show:
- You can transform **organizations** (Walmart)
