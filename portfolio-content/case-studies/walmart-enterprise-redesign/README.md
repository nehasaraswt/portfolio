# Case Study: Walmart Enterprise Redesign
## Scaling Design Leadership in Enterprise Organizations

### Overview
This folder contains all content and assets for the Walmart case study, positioned as a design leadership and organizational transformation project.

### Narrative Focus
- **Role**: Design Director
- **Challenge**: Building design practice from zero in Fortune 500 engineering-led org
- **Impact**: Transformed org culture, grew team from 1→12 designers, established design governance
- **Themes**: Team building, organizational change, design systems, enterprise design

## Content Writing Checklist

### Challenge Section
- [ ] Describe the business context (what was the situation?)
- [ ] Explain the organizational problem (not just product problem)
- [ ] State the mandate/goal you were given
- [ ] Use numbers: team size, timeline, impact area

**Example opening**:
```
Walmart is a Fortune 500 enterprise with 300+ engineers building 
internal tools. When I joined as Design Director, the organization's 
design culture was: features built first, "designed" afterward. This 
created significant UX debt and marginal product quality.
```

### Approach Section
- [ ] Explain your strategic thinking (not just tactical execution)
- [ ] Show key decisions and why you made them
- [ ] Mention team/org structure implications
- [ ] Be specific about phases or timeline

**Example**:
```
Rather than immediately redesigning products, I proposed a 3-phase 
organizational transformation:

Phase 1: Establish design authority through governance (design reviews,
standards, org structure that embeds design early in decisions)

Phase 2: Build capability through team growth and mentorship

Phase 3: Systematize through documentation and automation
```

### Results Section
- [ ] Quantify everything possible (%, growth, time saved, etc.)
- [ ] Include both team metrics AND product metrics
- [ ] Connect results back to strategic goals you set
- [ ] Be honest about timeline (change takes time)

**Example metrics**:
- Design system adoption: 89% of teams using standards
- Feature velocity: +34% faster to market
- Team growth: 1 → 12 designers
- Designers promoted to leads: 4
- Support tickets reduced: -28%

## Image Optimization Guidelines

### File Format
- **JPG** for photographs and realistic images
- **PNG** for diagrams and graphics (if transparency needed)
- **WebP** for web (with JPG fallback)

### Image Sizes
- **Hero image**: 1200x675px (1200x675@2x.jpg for retina = 2400x1350px)
- **Process images**: 1000x600px
- **Solution images**: 1000x600px or 800x600px (varies by layout)
- **Results images**: 800x400px (landscape) or 600x600px (square)

### Optimization
- Keep file sizes under 200-300KB per image
- Use TinyPNG or similar to optimize without quality loss
- Test images at 1x and 2x resolution on retina displays

## Questions to Answer While Writing

1. **Team**: How many people did you lead? What was the team structure?
2. **Timeline**: Over what period did this transformation happen?
3. **Metrics**: What improved as a result of your work?
4. **Decision-making**: What was the hardest decision you made?
5. **Delegation**: How did you empower others to own parts of this?
6. **Legacy**: What's still in place today because of work you did?
7. **Failure**: What didn't work? What did you learn?
8. **Impact**: Who was affected (users, business, org, team)?
