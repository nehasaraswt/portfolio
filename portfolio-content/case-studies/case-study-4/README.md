## How to Use This Template

### Step 1: Choose Your Third Project
Review the PDFs in `/leaves/` folder and select one that:
1. Demonstrates a clear strength or unique expertise
2. Shows measurable impact (team growth, product success, org change)
3. Complements Walmart (org transformation) and 247.ai (systems + emerging tech)

**Questions to ask yourself**:
- What's the business outcome?
- How big was the team you led?
- What was novel or challenging about this project?
- What did you learn that shaped your approach to future work?

### Step 2: Determine the Narrative
Use one of these frames:

#### Frame A: Design Systems (Complementary to 247.ai)
```
TITLE: "Design Systems at Scale"
FOCUS: How you've established design systems across different contexts
NARRATIVE: "I've led design system initiatives in 3+ organizations, 
each requiring different approaches."
BEST FOR: Proving depth in design systems expertise
```

#### Frame B: Emerging Tech (Different from 247.ai)
```
TITLE: "Design Innovation in [Technology Domain]"
FOCUS: How you approached design for new technology
NARRATIVE: "Beyond AI, I've led design initiatives for emerging tech..."
BEST FOR: Showing breadth of emerging tech expertise
```

#### Frame C: Product Strategy & User Impact
```
TITLE: "User-Centered Product Strategy at [Scale/Type]"
FOCUS: How design influenced product direction and user outcomes
NARRATIVE: "Design isn't just about interfaces; it's about product thinking"
BEST FOR: Showing strategic design thinking beyond org/systems
```

#### Frame D: Team & Cultural Transformation
```
TITLE: "Transforming Design Culture at [Company Type]"
FOCUS: How you built design capability and culture
NARRATIVE: "Different from Walmart, here's another org I transformed"
BEST FOR: Proving repeated ability to scale design teams and culture
```

### Step 3: Mirror the Walmart & 247.ai Structure

Follow the same image organization:
- `/images/hero/` — One strategic image (team, diagram, or product)
- `/images/process/` — 4-6 images showing your thinking/research/iteration
- `/images/solution/` — 4-6 images of design solutions/system
- `/images/results/` — 2-4 images with quantified impact

Use the same content sections:
- Challenge (problem statement)
- Approach (your strategic thinking)
- Process (how you worked)
- Results (quantified outcomes)
- Key Decisions (and why)
- Lessons Learned (what you'd do differently)

## Content Writing Path

### If You Choose Design Systems (Frame A):
```
NARRATIVE: Why this case study?
"While 247.ai was my first systems-at-scale initiative, [Project X] 
shows how I've applied systems thinking to [different context]."

UNIQUE ANGLE:
- Different scale? (smaller/larger)
- Different approach? (different methodology, tools, phases)
- Different outcomes? (different metrics that matter for this org)
- Different constraints? (mobile-first, accessibility-first, etc.)
```

### If You Choose Emerging Tech (Frame B):
```
NARRATIVE: Why this case study?
"Beyond AI, I've worked on design for [VR/AR/ML/Blockchain/etc.], 
where I applied similar pattern-creation methodology to [different domain]."

UNIQUE ANGLE:
- Different technology paradigm
- Different user expectations
- Different design challenges and solutions
- What you learned that applies across emerging tech domains
```

### If You Choose Product Strategy (Frame C):
```
NARRATIVE: Why this case study?
"My design leadership isn't just about org/systems—it's about using 
design to influence product strategy. [Project X] shows design-driven 
product thinking."

UNIQUE ANGLE:
- How design uncovered a different product direction
- User insights that changed product strategy
- Design as competitive advantage
- Metrics on product success (growth, retention, etc.)
```
